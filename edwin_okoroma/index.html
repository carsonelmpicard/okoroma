When Edwin Okoroma stepped on the pitch of Fetzer Field in the fall of 1963, he knew he was making history.

By playing on UNC’s men’s soccer team that season, the Nigerian-native became the first Black athlete in the history of the university. At a time when Franklin Street was still segregated and less than 100 African Americans attended the university, Okoroma was a pioneer, leading the way for future Black athletes and Black students to attend UNC.
